Growing Up With Pets

I started life with a positive association towards living with animals. When I was very young, my mom, dad, baby sister, and I lived on a small farm in Miscampbell, Ontario. We had two horses named Bo and Dee, a german shepherd named Tessie, and I have a vague memory of having chickens at some point.


Our dog was somewhat famous because she had run off one day to save the boy who lived a mile down the road. He had gotten himself between a momma bear and her cubs! For those of you who don’t already know this: baby bears are super cute and friendly, but going near them can get you killed. Seriously. Also, it is always best to have a dog with you if you are going out into the woods by yourself.


Eventually my parents split up. One of the horses had died and the other they sold. We moved into town. Tessie got old and went out to live with grandpa on his farm in Pinewood. No this isn’t one of those stories your parents tell you when your dog dies, we went out to visit them all the time. She lived there until she was 16 or so, when she died of old age.


Mom had developed allergies to dogs and cats, so we never got another furry animal at her house. But after my sister and I had begged enough for a new pet finally she broke down and took us to the pet store in Winnipeg (there were none closer than that). Mom had a cockatiel when she was growing up that she had loved so she led us towards the bird section. The girl working at the store showed us the budgies. She told us about how they had been playing with them and training them a bit. There was one in particular that had mastered the “step-up” command and she thought he was going to be very trainable and maybe even be able to talk. I loved him instantly. He was a blue budgie who I eventually named Blue Boy at auntie’s suggestion. My sister chose an all white budgie she named Snowflake. We got all the accessories we would need and set off for the four hour car ride home. The birds sat in their cage between us in the back seat. I still have this vivid memory of my aunt turning around in the passenger seat, and upon seeing my face saying, “Sarah is in love.”
